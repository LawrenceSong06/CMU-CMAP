Carnegie Mellon Action Project Resources

CMAP Provide various resources for minority students to support their academic life at Carnegie Mellon University

For academic support, we provide students services including tutoring sessions, awareness sessions, seminars, workshops, academic counseling sessions and special meetings arranged by the University, its colleges, and the C-MAP staff. (C-MAP -- Black Students on Campus [DIGITIZED], 1970-1971, p. 43)

We also help in connecting students by assigning minority students in the same dormitory area, and our advisors will emotionally care for the students and provide necessary support to them. We believe this can reinforce emotional and academic assistance between minority students. (C-MAP -- Black Students on Campus [DIGITIZED], 1970-1971, pp. 4-6)

Financially, we offer students scholarships and job opportunities during summer to relief the economy burden of minority students.

The August Program

The August Program is the first step in CMAP's education program. All CMAP Students are required to take the August Program. It is an intensive five-week program designed to prepare entering freshmen academically for the freshman curriculum at CMU and personally for university life. After the five weeks, the students will need to continue to attend the supervised studying time every Sunday evening.

In addition, the August Program is designed to introduce a large group of pre-college students to a highly quantitative academic experience at CMU and to introduce them to various fields of engineering at an early stage. For pre-college students, the program is only open for juniors and seniors. (Raczkiewicz, 1981)

The program provides students valuable training in mathematics, physics and chemistry. This traning is effective proved to be effective in improving students' first years academic performance.

(C-MAP -- Report, 1976-1978, pp. 8-9)

Academic Advising and Support

CMAP academic advising provides students accurate, up-to-date information and guidance which help the students make critical decisions leading to their B.S. degrees.

Our advising advisors will regularly contact with students' professors and other faculty members to keep track of the students. Our counselors will reach out to help the students if there is any difficulty identified. The counselors can discuss solutions mutually with students covering a wide range of problems, such as offering tutoring, designing efficient study plan, and solving personal problems. Students are also required to meet an CMAP academic counselor at the end of each semester to review the semester's work and plan for the upcoming semester.

Other than the semester checks, we also offer some more frequent, personalized helps to students including one-on-one tutoring, group help sessions, and scheduled help sessions, as follows.

One-on-one Tutoring Session

One-on-one tutoring session is a bi-weekly hold help session to solve problems reported by the related professors, staff members, or the student himself prior to the beginning of the semester. In the session, CMAP has a group of tutors, including the teachers, staff members, and upper-class CMAP students on call.

Group Help Sessions

Group help sessions is a lecture or study hall set up if several students are having the same difficulty. In the sessions, our staff will assist the students to solve the problems they encountered.

Scheduled Help Sessions

CMAP offers academic help sessions that can be scheduled by the students to flexibly deal with their difficulties in classes that are traditionally challenging. If students have any problems regarding these sections, they are welcomed to schedule a help session with a CMAP staff member.

Job Placement

CMAP helps students to find high-paying summer or permanent employment through its special relationships with certain corporations. The job placement opportunity is valuable for students not only because of the payment, but also because of the experience that benefits the students in long run.

(C-MAP -- Report, 1976-1978, p. 12)
