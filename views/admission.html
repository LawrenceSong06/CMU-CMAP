Admission to Carnegie Mellon Action Project

Why Should I Join CMAP?

In CMAP, we provide additional academic, social, and financial support to the minory students. Through providing these resources, we are able to correct the racial imbalance and enhance minority students' access to campus resources. By joining CMAP, minority students are more possible to achieve academic success in the university. (Sanday, Johnson, Hamilton, Mullins, 1968, p. 2)

What Do I Need to Apply to CMAP?

  • Call to schedule an interview with our admission consultants. Our admission team with three black consultants will conduct the interview in one of three community facilities near your neighborhood.
  • The information of your previous college experience.
  • (Optional) Your College Board, ACT, and other test data.
  • Your high school transcript.
  • (Optional) Upward-Bound experience and recommendations.
  • Your curriculum choice.
  • Your high school record, including attendence record.
  • A personal statement demonstrating your knowledge of school, counselors, and your family background.
(Sanday, Johnson, Hamilton, Mullins, 1968, p. 3)

What Do We Anticipate From You?

Be aware that our project is not an affirmative action program. We anticipate students to be highly motivated academically, which means you need to realize how much efforts you need to spend to be high achieving in Carnegie Mellon University. We also anticipate a student is having a good grade in their high school. (Sanday, Johnson, Hamilton, Mullins, 1968, p. 3) We also expect our students can be socially responsible. (C-MAP -- Black Students on Campus [DIGITIZED], 1970-1971, p. 42)
