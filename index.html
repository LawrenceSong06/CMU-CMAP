We Support Minority Students To Succeed

We are a big family that cares

CMAP is a program designed to facilitate minority students to success at Carnegie Mellon University. We provide academic advising, peer tutoring, scholarships, and many other resources to help you success. We also help to to connect to other resources offered for all students. (Raczkiewicz,1981,pp. 18-19)

Over the years, CMAP has made significant strides in empowering minority students across disciplines. From 1970 to 1976, we have successfully increased the retention rate of minority freshmen students from 73% to 91% in engineering and science and helped students to achiev very high QPA. (C-MAP -- Report, 1976-1978) Our comprehensive academic and social support system has helped many students achieve their academic and career goals.

Most importantly, we are a big family that cares for every member with warmth. We connect members, listen to them, motivate them, and empower them. Our faculty members are responsible and to every singe student in the program. In here, you will find the person who always support you in your journey.

Join the family, thrive here, and engage in building a brighter future of all minority students.

We Offer Many Resources for Your Success

  • The August Program

    CMAP offers a 5 weeks long program prepares students for college study before entering, providing students with neccessary fundations for their studies. (C-MAP -- Report, 1976-1978, pp. 8-9)

  • Advising & Support

    Our advisors gathers information from professors to keep track of every students and give students up-to-date information and advises to improve their performance. (C-MAP -- Report, 1976-1978, p. 10)

  • Personal Counseling

    Personal counseling teaches students a wide range of skills, including learning strategies, career planning, financial aids, medical topics, etc. (C-MAP -- Report, 1976-1978, p. 11)

  • Job Placement

    CMAP provides students chances to find high-paying job for summer and seeks for permanent employment in our cooperating corporations. (C-MAP -- Report, 1976-1978, p. 12)

Our Achievments

  • From 1977 to 1978, we successfully enrolled 140 minority students in CIT, successfully increased the representation of minority students in previously white-dominated schools. (C-MAP -- Report, 1976-1978, p. 22)
  • CMAP has also encouraged minority female students to enter new fields, including science and engineering. We enrolled 35 female minority students in CIT/MIS in 1978, comparing to 0 in 1968. Our trained minority women are among the most actively sought members of today's job market. (C-MAP -- Report, 1976-1978, p. 7)
  • CMAP graduates achieved great success in the job market--they have an employment of about 100% in a depressed job market. They are working in a variety of industries including Chemistry, Engineering, AMS, Psychology, English, Economics, Drama, Music, and Design. (C-MAP -- Report, 1976-1978, p. 15)
  • Our program also enhanced the colleges' influence in local community. 20% of our students come from Pittsburgh area and another 20% of our students come from Pennsyvania. (Raczkiewicz, 1976-1978, pp. 18-19)
